Privacy Policy

App: Slenora
Effective date: 19 May 2026

Slenora is a sleep analytics mobile application operated by Ridith Shetty in India. This Privacy Policy explains how Slenora collects, uses, stores, and discloses information when users access or use the app.

Information Collected

Depending on how the app is used, Slenora may collect or process the following information:

  • Google sign-in information, limited to the user's name and email address.
  • Sleep-related information such as sleep start time, sleep end time, sleep duration, sleep disturbances, sleep scores, sleep history, and related analysis results.
  • User-submitted content, including sleep notes entered by the user.
  • Technical and account-related information necessary to operate the account and synchronize saved history using Supabase.

Microphone and Audio Processing

Slenora uses microphone access during sleep to detect snoring and analyze sleep-related audio patterns. Raw microphone audio is processed on the device for snoring detection and is not intended to be stored as part of the app's sleep history.

Sleep analysis results, including scores, summaries, detected events, and historical records, may be stored in Supabase so users can access past sleep sessions inside the app.

Where Data Is Stored

Slenora processes raw snoring-detection audio on the device for analysis and does not intend to store raw microphone recordings as part of sleep history. Account-related information, sleep analysis results, summaries, detected events, notes, and saved sleep history may be stored using Supabase infrastructure so users can access their data inside the app.

What Slenora Does Not Collect

Slenora is not intended to collect payment card information through the app. It is also not intended to store raw microphone recordings as part of normal sleep history storage unless the app is later changed and the policy is updated accordingly.

How Information Is Used

  • To provide sleep tracking, snoring detection, analysis, score generation, and history features.
  • To authenticate users through Google sign-in.
  • To save and display past sleep sessions and related sleep insights.
  • To respond to support, privacy, and deletion requests.
  • To improve core app functionality, stability, and user experience.

Legal Basis and Consent

Where required, Slenora relies on user consent and other lawful bases permitted under applicable law to process personal data. Microphone access and other sensitive permissions should be used only after clear in-app disclosure and user consent.

Third-Party Services

Slenora uses Supabase for backend and data storage functionality. Third-party services may process information as needed to support app features.

Data Sharing

Slenora does not sell personal data. Information may be shared only as reasonably necessary with service providers supporting operation of the app, or when required by law, regulation, legal process, or lawful government request.

Data Retention

Account information and saved sleep history may be retained for as long as reasonably necessary to provide the app's features, maintain account history, comply with legal obligations, resolve disputes, or enforce legal terms. If deletion is requested, relevant information will be deleted within a reasonable period unless retention is required by law.

Account and Data Deletion

Users may request deletion of their account and associated stored data by contacting slenora.app@gmail.com.

Children's Privacy

Slenora is intended for a general audience and is not specifically directed to children under 13.

Data Security

Reasonable steps are taken to protect stored information, but no method of electronic storage or transmission is completely secure, and absolute security cannot be guaranteed.

International Users

The app is operated from India. If users access the app from other regions, their information may be processed and stored in jurisdictions where the app's service providers operate.

Changes to This Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time to reflect app changes, legal requirements, or operational changes. The updated version becomes effective when posted unless a different date is stated.
